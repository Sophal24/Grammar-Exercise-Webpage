1. Simple Future Tense

Form: Subject + will + infinitive without to

The simple future is used :

1. To predict a future event
Ex: It will rain tomorrow.

2. To express willingness
Ex: I will do the washing-up.

3. To make an offer
Ex: Shall I open the window?

4. To give order
Ex: You will do exactly as I say.

2. Future Continuous

Form: Subject + simple future of the verb 'to be' + present participle

The used:

1. Project ourselves into the future.
Ex: This time next week, I will be sun-bathing in Bali.

2. Predicting or guessing about future events.
Ex: He'll be coming to the meeting, I expect.

3. Ask politely for the information about the future.
Ex: Will Jim be coming with us?

3. Future Perfect

Form: Subject + will have + past participle of the main verb

It is used for:

The future perfect tense refers to a completed action in the future. When we use this tense we are projecting ourselves forward into the future and looking back at an action that will be completed some time later than now. It is most often used with a time expression.

Ex: I will Have been here for six months on June 23rd.
Ex: By the time you read this, I will have left.

4. Future Perfect Continuous

Form: Subject + will have been + present participle

We used this for:

Like the future perfect simple, this form is used to project ourselves forward in time and to look back. It refers to events or actions that are currently unfinished but will be finished at some future time. It is most often used with a time expression.

Ex: I will have been waiting here for three hours by six o'clock.
